Assignment 4: XPath pratice

Question 1:How would you isolate the ID number of this encoded text (VAA4025-109-1-a16)?

XPath: /TEI.2/text/body/div/@id

Question 2: How many lists are in the article text?

Answer: 1 XPath:

XPath: count(/TEI.2/text//list)

Question 3: How would you isolate the copyright statement (Copyright 2013 Trustees of Indiana University)?

XPath: /TEI.2/teiHeader/fileDesc/publicationStmt/availability

Question 4: What are two different ways to isolate the publisher and pubPlace elements together (Indiana University Libraries Bloomington, IN)?

This one stumped me a bit. The first way seems pretty straightfoward, the second way seems rather cumbersome, so I am not sure if that is really what you were looking for.

Options:

  1. //publicationStmt/publisher | //publicationStmt/pubPlace
  2. //publicationStmt/child::text()[position()=1] | //publicationStmt/child::text()[position()=2]

Question 5: How many paragraphs are in the text of this article?

Answer: 34

XPath: count(/TEI.2/text//p)

Question 6: What different states are mentioned in the text?

Answer: Indiana, Ohio, South Carolina, Kentucky, Arkansas, North Carolina

XPath: distinct-values(/TEI.2/text//placeName/region [@type="state"])

Explanation: I would have probably gotten a better answer if I could have looked for region type="state" and has distinct @key values, but I could not quite figure out the syntax. For some reason Monroe County is tagged as a state, and Indiana appears as both Indiana and Ind.

Question 7: How many distinct cities are mentioned in the text? Hint: use distinct-values()

Answer: 25

XPath: count(distinct-values(/TEI.2/text//placeName/settlement [@type="city"]))

Question 8: How many times does "Evansville" appear in this article? Hint: use ctrl+F to check your work

Answer: 2

Count(/TEI.2/text//placeName[contains(., "Evansville")])

Explanation: Talks about Evansville in one book review. Evansville is mentioned in the title and the review itself. Evansville also shows up twice more in this section as the value of the attribute @reg

Question 9: What's your best guess for how to figure out how many book reviews there are in this article using XPath alone?

Answer: 22

XPath: Count(//p/hi[@rend="i"])

Explanation: This really counts how many books are reviewed, because some reviews cover more than one book at a time.
